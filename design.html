Giftr

A notable highlight of my summer interning at Capital One was the Carbon intern hackathon, where my team clinched first prize in the API category! We made a Django application that crowd sources gift ideas and also integrates Capital One's DevExchange Rewards API. For more about the experience, you can refer to this blog post written by my teammate that they published on Medium, as well as check out the repo (featuring a very extensive readme)!

It was fun getting to use Python again, which I hadn't really touched since my 2013 Defender project, as well as learning the ropes of Django which I found less painful than Rails.

PRC Consulting

As part of a class that partners with local Pittsburgh nonprofits to help improve their business and operations, my team worked with the Pennsylvania Resources Council to collect and analyze data at their recycling events. We served as consultants for the project for fifteen weeks and had a great time getting to know our clients and their mission.

Our contributions included:
  • -Setup, documentation, and integration of an online survey tool
  • -Testing and deployment at their real waste collection event
  • -Developing a mapping events platform for their website
As my first foray into consulting for a real organization, this was a fantastic and rewarding experience that demonstrated the importance of communication and professionalism outside the context of the classroom. Good results came from being flexible and transparent to make sure that the clients were involved in every step of the process and could give the feedback we needed to deliver what they wanted.

A&M Bread Express

Fully functional mock retail bread retail store. The site was a product of a semester's worth of cumulative project work, beginning with the ERD design, transitioning into the model and backend setup, and topping it off with the front end. Fantastic experience with full stack development using Ruby on Rails, creating a robust web application.

Features include:
  • -Account creation, different account types, user authentication and authorization
  • -Viewable and modifiable customer, address, product information (restricted to certain users)
  • -Adding/removing from cart, checking out order, credit card authentication
  • -Responsive design with Materialize framework

Defender

A side-scrolling space shooter written from scratch in Python with the Pygame module. Done over the course of three weeks as a class term project, it was a great exercise in pacing and object oriented design. Plus tons of fun to modify sprites and pay homage to retro games!

Features include:
  • -5 types of power-ups to collect, such as a smart bomb, laser augmentation, and speed
  • -5 types of enemies with different movement patterns and abilities
  • -Ramped difficulty and scoring system
  • -High score board
